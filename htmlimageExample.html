The Origin of Flower Names

Flower names originate from various sources, including Latin, Greek, and other languages. Many flower names botanical origins, derived from the plant's scientific name or descriptive features. For example, "Rosa" is derived from the Latin word for "rose," while "Dahlia" honors the Swedish botanist Andreas Dahl. Some flower names have mythological origins, such as "Iris," named after the Greek goddess of the rainbow. Others have historical or cultural significance, like "Lotus," which holds great importance in various Eastern cultures. Additionally, flower names can be derived from the names of people, as a way of honoring botanists, explorers, or other individuals who made significant contributions to the field of botany. The etymology and origin of each flower name may vary, adding depth and richness to the world of floral nomenclature.
